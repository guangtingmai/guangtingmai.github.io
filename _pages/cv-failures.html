Academic setbacks (2009-2013) after leaving my hometown started from consecutive failed (naively, not-even-anywhere-near) MPhil/PhD applications in Hong Kong three years in a row followed by an MSc application to Canada in the fourth year due to substandard undergrad performances; and one to the UK in the fifth (possibly due to an ultra-late submission in March/April).

By the end of PhD (2019-2020), four project grant applications aiming to secure a postdoc position were rejected (all at the first round) alongside another four consecutive interview failures before starting the first postdoc in the midst of the Pandemic. I failed at a faulty application a year later (sent out without feedback).

Meanwhile I failed at five fellowship applications - three before/during the Pandemic (Sir Henry Wellcome, British Academy, NIHR) (2019-2020); two afterwards (Wellcome ECA, MRC CDA) (2024).

More to come. Failures are frustrating but may not always be bad since sometimes ECRs may be able to gather stronger support afterwards. Keep Calm and Carry On!
