--- layout: archive title: "Child-mother inter-brain synchrony" permalink: /interbrain-sync/ author_profile: true ---

We performed studies using fNIRS ‘hyperscanning’ (simultaneous recording of brain activities from more than one participant) during social interactions (both verbal and non-verbal) between typically developing children and their mothers during naturalistic toy playing at a table. We demonstrated the success in employing phase transfer entropy, which captures directionalities during interactions (child-led vs mother-led) compared to more conventional methods (e.g., wavelet coherence), to measure inter-brain synchrony:

Mai, G., Papoutselou, E., Harrison, S., Mai, G., Buck, B., Patil, N., Wiggins, I., & Hartley, D. (2024). Investigating mother–child inter‐brain synchrony in a naturalistic paradigm: A functional near infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS) hyperscanning study. European Journal of Neuroscience, 59(6):1386-1403.

While hyperscanning is also being applied in cochlear-implanted children, a recent NIHR funded efficacy trial of a speech and language therapy in CI children will examine how child-mother inter-brain synchrony changes following the therapy.
